## Rope guide after the drum

Reason:

The rope enters from the right.

The drum is placed before the level-wind.

This maximizes the distance between drum and guide,
reducing the fleet angle and improving winding accuracy.



## Directional Safety

Maintaining the correct flight direction toward the winch is one of the pilot's primary responsibilities.

A pilot who flies away from the tow line experiences an increasing sideways component of the rope force.

As the angle increases:

- the lateral pull increases,
- the pilot must make larger steering corrections,
- recovery becomes progressively more difficult,
- the situation can rapidly become unstable.

An experienced tow operator recognizes this development early and immediately reduces the commanded tow force.

Reducing the tow force reduces both the forward and lateral rope forces, allowing the pilot to regain directional control.

If the deviation continues to increase, the tow shall be terminated.

---

## 90° Rule

If the pilot reaches approximately **90 degrees** from the intended tow direction, the tow line shall be cut immediately.

At this point the tow has become unsafe.

Continuing the tow can rapidly increase the danger to both the pilot and surrounding people.

The release decision is based on the operator's observation of the pilot and wing, not on the measured tow force.

Immediate release always has priority over completing the tow.
